📖 Learn: Comma Splice explained

A comma splice joins two independent clauses with only a comma. Independent clauses have a subject + verb and can stand alone.

✖️ Wrong: I like tea, it is refreshing.
✔️ Fixed: I like tea. It is refreshing. (period) or I like tea; it is refreshing. (semicolon) or I like tea, for it is refreshing. (conjunction)

15 examples of comma splices

  • The sun is shining, we should go out.
  • She loves reading, she has many books.
  • He finished his work, he went home.
  • I enjoy hiking, it keeps me fit.
  • We were late, the movie already started.
  • She speaks three languages, she is multilingual.
  • He didn't study, he failed the exam.
  • I called you, you didn't answer.
  • They arrived early, they got good seats.
  • She is kind, everyone likes her.
  • The car broke down, we had to walk.
  • I love music, it makes me happy.
  • He was tired, he went to bed.
  • She wrote a poem, it was beautiful.
  • We missed the bus, we took a taxi.
